unlock efficiency & savings: outsource your salesforce needs to a multi-disciplinary expert

It's interesting how every role - be it an admin, a developer, or an architect - brings a unique perspective to Salesforce tasks.

administrator is usually focused on managing and customizing the platform to meet user needs. They ensure the system is user-friendly, streamlined, and maintainable for everyday business operations.

developer is someone who builds and modifies the system, often coding custom solutions and integrations. They need to ensure the system is optimized and can handle these customizations without impacting its overall performance.

architect is responsible for the overall design and structure of the system, ensuring it's scalable, reliable, and secure. They look at the big picture and plan how different components of Salesforce will interact with each other and with external systems.

    new org creation

    Plain and simple. If you have a new org and it's blank, you'll naturally want to tailor it to your business and implement your specific processes. This task calls for architectural knowledge and skills because there are several important factors to consider:

  • What will the data model look like?
  • Which integration pattern do I need for external services?
  • What will my security model look like?
  • In which processes should we opt for clicks instead of codes?

    • The key point to remember is that once your new org is set up, it's mostly going to stay the same. Once data begins to populate and users start adjusting to the process, it becomes challenging to alter the processes and data model.
